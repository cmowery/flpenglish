What has been done

During the last year, I’ve had a number of incredible experiences, but the ones that stand out the most are those with my friends. Through FLP, I’ve found genuine people I truly enjoy being around and that I trust. Throughout high school, I had a bunch of friends that betrayed my trust and alienated me during my junior year, and consequently trusting others was very hard for me, but I made relationships with others who were willing to invest in me, and I was able to invest myself in them. It’s created a very comforting college experience, and has helped my year become what it is. When Mr. Thesien came to speak, he fundamentally changed my FLP experience, and inspired me for the first time all year. I wrote the following letter expressing my gratitude:

Mr. Thesien,

During the week leading up to your talk, we were warned it would likely go over, and consequently, a lot of people were talking about using their ‘skip’ that week. Myself included. As I walked down the mall, I was contemplating whether or not to skip it at the last second, but I didn’t, and in the end, I didn't. I wholeheartedly believe that your conversation was the best we’ve had the entire year, and most of my peers who went agreed. That being said, some students did skip, but those who didn’t were incredibly grateful they went, have constantly ‘rubbed’ your talk into them, and we still talk about you and what you said in our classes, almost a month later. Almost my entire English class referenced you and your experiences during our last assignment — around a month after you spoke to us. You are constantly a topic of conversation among us as students, and we all seem to have learned several things from you.

I made my bed the Wednesday after you talked to us. And the day after. And the day after. I’ve made my bed every single day since you spoke. I’ve finally taken my first steps to make my dreams for after University to become a reality. I got a job here on campus, and that job has offered me a potential internship position here over the summer. That was 12 days ago. Your openness inspired me to take the steps I’d been talking about taking for over a year now, and so I guess what I’m really trying to say is Thank you. You were a joy to listen to. You made an hour feel like five minutes, and that’s a rare feat nowadays.

My name is Cole Mowery, and I’m dreaming of making a company which creates corporate media. If we could, I’d love to stay in touch. At the bottom of my email are all the places you can find me.

Once again, I just wanted to say thank you for inspiring me to take the step I’d been talking about for two years now. I’m sure you’ve impacted my entire class, but I KNOW you’ve impacted me.

My favorite FLP experience I had this year was to create the video for Precious Memories. I had the opportunity to make a new video for our Thanksgiving Dinner event, and while the other video was good, it didn’t have the professionalism that I wanted to add. I was ultimately able to create a video that was good, and created a new experience for the people it was intended for, I believe that I could have made it better in retrospect. I feel that it was too long for what it was trying to say, or at the very least repetitive. This embodies the things I find to be very important, and especially the idea that I learn from failure. While I didn’t fail in this situation, I definitely believe that I could have done a better job, and will carry these concerns into any future video project I create.
